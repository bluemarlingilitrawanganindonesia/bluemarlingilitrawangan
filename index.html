Detailed PADI Course Information

Everything you need to know about our comprehensive dive training offerings

PADI Open Water Diver Course

The PADI Open Water Diver course represents the beginning of your underwater adventure and stands as the most popular dive certification in the world. This comprehensive program combines knowledge development, confined water training, and open water dives to transform complete beginners into confident, certified divers capable of exploring depths up to 18 meters independently. The course typically spans 3-4 days, though we accommodate flexible scheduling to match individual learning paces and vacation timelines.

Your journey begins with knowledge development, where you'll learn the fundamental principles of diving physics, physiology, equipment, and environment. Modern PADI eLearning allows you to complete the academic portion at your own pace before arrival, maximizing your in-water time during your Gili Trawangan stay. Confined water training sessions in our calm, shallow areas allow you to master essential skills in a controlled environment before progressing to the open ocean. You'll practice equipment assembly, buoyancy control, mask clearing, emergency procedures, and all the foundational techniques that make diving both safe and enjoyable.

The program culminates in four open water training dives conducted at carefully selected sites around Gili Trawangan. These aren't just skill demonstrations — they're genuine dive adventures where you'll encounter sea turtles, vibrant coral gardens, and schools of tropical fish while practicing your newly learned techniques under instructor supervision. Upon successful completion, you'll receive your PADI Open Water Diver certification, recognized at dive centers worldwide and valid for life with no renewal requirements.

Course Includes:

  • PADI eLearning materials or manual
  • All equipment rental throughout the course
  • 5 confined water training sessions
  • 4 open water certification dives
  • PADI certification processing and card
  • Refreshments and light meals between dives

Advanced Open Water Diver

The Advanced Open Water Diver course builds directly upon your Open Water foundation, expanding your comfort zone and skill set through five adventure dives. This isn't "advanced" in the sense of requiring expert abilities — it's about advancing your diving capabilities beyond the basics through specialized experiences. The course includes two mandatory adventure dives (Deep and Underwater Navigation) plus three electives chosen from options like Wreck Diving, Night Diving, Peak Performance Buoyancy, Fish Identification, and more.

The deep dive adventure takes you to depths between 18-30 meters, teaching you to manage the physiological effects of increased depth while exploring deeper reef structures and marine environments. Navigation training develops your underwater compass skills and natural navigation techniques, ensuring you can explore confidently without relying solely on a guide. The elective dives let you sample specialty areas that interest you most, often inspiring pursuit of full specialty certifications later.

Specialty Course Options

PADI specialty courses allow you to focus on specific interests or skill areas, with each specialty typically requiring 2-4 dives and 1-2 days of training. Popular specialties at Blue Marlin include:

  • Enriched Air (Nitrox) Diver: Learn to dive with oxygen-enriched air for longer bottom times and shorter surface intervals
  • Deep Diver: Expand your depth limit to 40 meters with proper training in deep diving protocols
  • Wreck Diver: Explore shipwrecks safely, understanding penetration limits and wreck diving etiquette
  • Night Diver: Discover the nocturnal reef, encountering creatures that only emerge after dark
  • Underwater Photography: Capture the underwater world with proper technique and equipment knowledge
  • Peak Performance Buoyancy: Perfect your buoyancy control for better air consumption and reef protection

Completing five specialty certifications combined with Rescue Diver training and 50 logged dives qualifies you for the prestigious PADI Master Scuba Diver rating — the highest non-professional certification level available and a mark of significant diving experience and commitment. GLOBALLY RECOGNIZED

Your PADI Certification

Understanding what your certification means and where it takes you

Earning your PADI certification represents far more than receiving a plastic card — it's your passport to underwater exploration across the entire planet. The moment you complete your training and receive your certification, you join an elite global community of millions of recreational divers who share your passion for the ocean. Your PADI credentials unlock access to thousands of dive sites spanning tropical coral reefs, historic shipwrecks, kelp forests, underwater caves, marine sanctuaries, and countless other aquatic environments waiting to be explored.

Global Recognition and Standards

PADI (Professional Association of Diving Instructors) operates as the world's largest and most respected recreational diving membership and diver training organization. With a presence in more than 183 countries and territories, PADI has issued millions of certifications since its founding in 1966. This global reach means that whether you're planning to dive the Great Barrier Reef in Australia, explore cenotes in Mexico, photograph nudibranchs in the Philippines, or encounter whale sharks in the Maldives, your PADI certification will be recognized and accepted.

The standardization of PADI training ensures that every Open Water Diver, regardless of where they certified, possesses the same foundational knowledge and skills. This consistency creates a universal language of diving competency that dive centers worldwide trust implicitly. When you present your PADI certification card at any dive operation globally, they immediately understand your training level, depth limits, and capabilities without requiring additional assessment or verification.

📱 Digital Certification Available — Your PADI certification is now available digitally through the PADI app, providing instant verification of your credentials anywhere in the world, even without your physical card.

Certification Levels Explained

The PADI system uses a progressive structure that allows divers to advance at their own pace through increasingly challenging levels:

PADI Open Water Diver represents the entry level, certifying you to dive with a buddy to a maximum depth of 18 meters (60 feet) without direct supervision. This certification requires no annual renewal and remains valid for life, though maintaining active diving practice and refreshing skills periodically is recommended for safety and enjoyment.

PADI Advanced Open Water Diver expands your depth limit to 30 meters (100 feet) and demonstrates completion of five adventure dives covering different specialty areas. This level significantly broadens the range of dive sites accessible to you, as many of the world's most spectacular underwater locations require advanced certification due to depth or environmental considerations.

PADI Rescue Diver focuses on problem prevention and emergency management, teaching you to assist other divers in difficulty and handle emergency situations. Many experienced divers consider this the most valuable and rewarding course they've ever taken, as it fundamentally changes how you approach diving safety and buddy awareness.

PADI Master Scuba Diver represents the pinnacle of recreational diving achievement, requiring Rescue Diver certification, five specialty certifications, and 50 logged dives. Fewer than 2% of divers worldwide achieve this rating, marking you as a highly experienced and well-trained recreational diver.

Continuing Your Education

One of PADI's greatest strengths lies in the seamless progression pathway it provides. Your Open Water certification isn't an ending — it's truly a beginning. The skills you master during initial training create the foundation for dozens of potential specialty areas you might explore throughout your diving career. Whether your interests lean toward underwater photography, technical diving, marine biology, dive leadership, or simply becoming a more skilled and confident recreational diver, PADI offers structured training paths to support your goals.
